The People: permanent staff and work studies

The scene shop is place like no other on campus and one of things that makes it so special is it's work environment. The people that work in the scene shop take pride in their work and are passionate about teaching new skills to students and accomodating anyone who passes through there walls. The scene shop is a community that welcomes all ideas and people and always has time for fun - whether it be trying hot dog flavored candy corn at Halloween, listening to Mario Kart video game music for hours, or ppeople watching from the dance studio.

Meet Our Staff

Fritz Bernstein

Technical Director, he/him/his

Fritz is a technical director and scenic carpenter with over 30 years in the industry. He has worked across the country at theaters such as Peninsula Players and at many theater houses in Chicago. He has also worked for the University of Wisconsin school system as a Technical Director. He loves cheese and hates writing bios.

Savannah Relos

Assistant Technical Director and Shop Supervisor, she/her/hers

Savannah is relatively new to Cornell, having just started in the position August 2021. She has worked at several theaters around the country including Triad Stage, Santa Fe Opera, Dallas Theatre Center and Florida Repertory. She has enjoyed this new foray into educational theater and cares deeply about her work study students' fingers and keeping them attached to their bodies.

Tim Ostrander

Properties Master he/him/his

Tim has been the Schwartz Center Props Coordinator since 2001. In that time he has propped and dressed the sets for several hundred shows as well as designing scenery for and performing in multiple PMA productions. Prior to that he lived in San Francisco and New York City where he performed onstage as a sketch, improv and stand-up comic while also working behind the scenes in television, commercial and theater production on projects for Comedy Central, Nickelodeon, HBO and PBS among many others.

Work Study Students

Work study students have the opportunity to work with industry professionals on projects that happen in and around the Schwartz Center. These students come in with some knowledge and experience as theater technicians and further hone their skills in theatrical and film construction, metal fabrication, set dressing, scenic painting, properties construction and more. Work study students are pivotal in the creation of these projects for the PMA department.
