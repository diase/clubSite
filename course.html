PMA 1610: Production Technology Laboratory

Course Description

This technology lab will provide students with a foundation of the production process through experiential learning of scenographic practices. Students will learn about the technical production processes as they pertain to:

  • scenery fabrication and installation
  • properties fabrication
  • costume fabrication, and
  • lighting installation and understanding of lighting equipment (primarily lighting for live performance).

This course fulfills the PMA major Production (technology) Lab requirement, and also the Production Lab requirement of the PMA, Dance, Film, and Theatre Minors. This course, in conjunction with other PMA courses, is intended to provide students with knowledge and practical experience in regards to the functionality and importance of scenographic elements.

Structure of Course

Class rotates between the Scene Shop and the Costume Shop depending on day of the week. In the Scene Shop portion students will receive hands on experience in the scenic carpentry, props and lighting departments, learning how to build scenery, assisting with painting scenery, hanging and focusing lights and more depending on the current production.

Course Meeting Times

  • Tuesday and Thursday 12:25-2:20
  • Office Hours by appointment

Skills Students have Acquired

  • Have gained practical knowledge as it pertains to the “behind the scenes” processes of technical production through lab sessions in a variety of technical areas.
  • Have actively engaged in the praxis of technical production by participating in hands-on work in the scenic & properties, lighting and costume areas.
  • Gain a practical vocabulary of production and technical elements as they pertain to technical production for live and mediated performance.
  • Be able to communicate effectively in regards to technical production processes.
  • Be able to effectively communicate with collaborators. Be able to respectfully & constructively respond and discuss with collaborators.
  • Be able to comprehend design materials as they are intended for production.
  • Have a richer “collaborator took kit” as it pertains to technical production.
  • Gain an appreciation for the technical processes as they pertain to Performance and Production
